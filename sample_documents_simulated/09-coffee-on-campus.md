# Grindhouse Café vs. Bean Scene: the campus coffee debate

**u/espresso_yourself:** The two real coffee spots on campus are **Grindhouse Café** (in
the library) and **Bean Scene** (in the student union). They're different vibes for
different needs.

**Grindhouse Café — the study coffee.** Inside Tewell Library, ground floor. Best actual
espresso on campus, real beans, baristas who know what they're doing. It's where you go when
you're settling in for a long library session. Downside: the line gets brutal at 9–10am and
again around 2pm, and it closes when the library's quiet floors are still full (around 9pm).
Takes dining dollars. Get the cold brew, it's excellent.

**Bean Scene — the convenience coffee.** In the union, faster, more of a grab-and-go chain
feel. The coffee is fine, not special, but the line moves and they have more food (bagels,
muffins, the breakfast sandwich). Open earlier than Grindhouse and located where everyone's
already walking, so it's the practical morning stop between classes. Also takes dining dollars.

**u/quadrat_kid:** Grindhouse if you care about the coffee, Bean Scene if you care about the
time. I do Bean Scene on the way to my 9am because I'm not waiting 12 minutes at Grindhouse,
but if I'm camping in the library all afternoon I'll do a Grindhouse cold brew.

**u/late_plate:** Note neither is open super late. Grindhouse closes ~9pm, Bean Scene ~8pm.
If you need caffeine at midnight for a study grind, the union convenience store sells cans
and there's a coffee machine in the 24-hour Engineering lounge.

**u/espresso_yourself:** Loyalty tip: Grindhouse does a punch card, tenth drink free, and
they honor it across the semester. Over a finals season that's real money.
