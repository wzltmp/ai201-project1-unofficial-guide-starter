# Meal Plan Megathread: which plan should I actually buy?

**u/budget_brain (OP):** Every year people overpay for the unlimited plan out of fear.
Here's the actual math. Brightwood has three plans:

- **Unlimited:** swipe in as many times as you want, ~$2,950/semester.
- **Block 175:** 175 swipes for the semester (about 12/week), ~$2,300/semester.
- **Commuter 80:** 80 swipes, ~$1,150, for people who aren't on campus every day.

A semester is ~15 weeks. Unlimited only makes sense if you actually eat **2 to 3
dining-hall meals a day, most days**. If you do, unlimited is cheaper per meal. But most
students don't — they skip breakfast, grab coffee, eat off campus on weekends. If you eat
about 2 meals a day on weekdays and bail on weekends, you land around 10–12 swipes/week,
which is the Block 175 sweet spot at $650 less.

**u/tofu_scramble:** This is exactly right. I had unlimited freshman year and tracked it —
I averaged 11 swipes a week because I never made it to breakfast. I switched to Block 175
sophomore year and saved real money. Unlimited is a tax on optimism about your morning self.

**u/late_plate:** One nuance: unlimited's hidden value is that you can swipe in just for a
coffee and a snack between classes without "spending" a meal, because there's no cap. If
you're someone who grazes — popping into the hall 4 times a day for small stuff — unlimited
can actually win even at a lower meal count. It's about visit frequency, not just meals.

**u/commuter_caroline:** Commuters: do NOT buy a residential plan. The Commuter 80 plus
dining dollars is the move. I'm on campus 4 days a week, eat one meal there, and 80 swipes
covers the semester with room to spare. Anything bigger is money you won't use because
swipes don't roll over.

**u/budget_brain:** TL;DR — **Unlimited** if you eat 2–3 hall meals daily or graze
constantly. **Block 175** for the typical "I eat lunch and dinner on weekdays" student;
it's the best value for most people. **Commuter 80** if you're not here every day. When in
doubt, you can downgrade after week 2 but not upgrade, so start lower.
