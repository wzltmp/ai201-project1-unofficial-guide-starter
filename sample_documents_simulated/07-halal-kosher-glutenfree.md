# Halal / kosher / gluten-free on campus — what's actually available?

**u/halal_eats:** For halal: The Marketplace grill carries halal chicken daily and labels
it, and North Quad does a halal entrée at dinner a few nights a week (check the app menu, it
rotates). Hillside is hit or miss — the staff are nice about telling you what's halal but
there's no dedicated station. The student-run Muslim Student Association keeps a shared
doc of which stations are reliably halal each week if you want the real-time info.

**u/shabbat_table:** Kosher is more limited. There's a dedicated **kosher kitchen at
Hillside Commons** (separate prep area, certified) that does lunch and dinner on weekdays
and Shabbat meals you sign up for through Hillel. It's the only certified-kosher hot food on
campus, so if you keep strict kosher, your meal plan basically has to route through Hillside.
The other halls have packaged kosher options in the grab-and-go fridges but nothing hot.

**u/celiac_sam:** Gluten-free: North Quad and The Marketplace both have dedicated GF
stations / labeled GF lines, and there's a separate gluten-free toaster at North Quad (huge
for cross-contamination). The dining site lets you filter the menu by allergen. Riverside is
the weakest for GF — small kitchen, more shared surfaces, ask staff before trusting a label.
The grain bowls at The Marketplace are an easy reliable GF lunch.

**u/halal_eats:** Adding: dining services will do a one-on-one with you if you have strict
requirements — email the dietitian and they'll walk you through every station. Worth doing in
your first week. The labeling has gotten a lot better but a five-minute meeting saves you a
semester of guessing.
