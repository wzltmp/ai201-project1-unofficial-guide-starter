# Riverside Hall review: small, quiet, and the best breakfast on campus

Riverside is the smallest of the four dining halls and it's tucked over by the river dorms,
so unless you live on that side you probably never go. That's a shame, because Riverside
does a few things better than anywhere else.

**Breakfast is the star.** Riverside's breakfast is the best on campus — real maple syrup,
made-to-order eggs, and the only hall that does fresh waffles on weekdays, not just
weekends. Because it's small and out of the way, the breakfast crowd is thin, so you can
walk in at 8:30 on a Tuesday and sit right down. If you're a breakfast person and you don't
mind the walk, Riverside beats the central halls easily.

**Lunch and dinner are limited.** The tradeoff for being small is fewer stations. There's
one rotating entrée, a deli station, and a salad bar — that's basically it. No stir-fry, no
big grill. Vegetarians will find the salad bar and not much else, so it's not the spot if
you have dietary restrictions (go to North Quad instead).

**Atmosphere.** This is the calmest, quietest hall. No lunch-rush chaos like Hillside. River
views from the window seats. A lot of people come here specifically to eat alone and read.
If you want a peaceful meal, Riverside wins; if you want variety, it doesn't.

**Hours.** Shorter than the others — roughly 7:30am to 7pm, closed between meals on
weekends. Check the app before you make the walk, because nothing's worse than getting to
Riverside and finding it closed between lunch and dinner.

**Verdict:** Go for breakfast, go for quiet. Skip it if you want selection.
