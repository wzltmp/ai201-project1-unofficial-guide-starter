# The Brightwood Vegan & Vegetarian Survival Guide

Compiled by the Veg Society. Where to actually eat well on a plant-based diet, ranked.

## 1. North Quad Dining — your home base
North Quad is the best dining hall for vegans and vegetarians, no contest. The
make-to-order **stir-fry / wok station** with pressed tofu is the most reliable hot vegan
meal on campus — you control what goes in the bowl, and it's a real cooked meal, not a side.
North Quad also labels allergens clearly and almost always has a vegan soup. If you only
learn one hall, learn this one. Bonus: it's open latest (until midnight weeknights), so
late-night vegan food is actually possible here.

## 2. The Marketplace — grain bowls and the fridge
The grain-bowl station at The Marketplace is easily customized vegan and is a great lunch.
The grab-and-go fridge usually stocks a couple of vegan wraps and hummus boxes. Watch the
pizza — the "cheese" is not vegan unless the sign specifically says so.

## 3. Hillside Commons — fine, with effort
Hillside has a salad bar and a pasta station where you can do marinara + veggies, plus a
rotating vegetarian entrée that's vegan maybe half the time. It's survivable but you'll eat
a lot of pasta. The lunch rush makes assembling a plant plate slow.

## 4. Riverside Hall — salad bar and pray
Riverside is the weakest for plant-based eaters — basically the salad bar. Great breakfast
(oatmeal, fruit, bagels) but lunch/dinner options are thin. Skip it if veg is your priority.

## General tips
- The wok line at North Quad closes ~30 min before the hall does — don't show up at the last
  minute expecting tofu.
- Ask for the allergen binder at any hall; staff will tell you what's vegan.
- "Vegetarian" labels at Brightwood are reliable; "vegan" labels less so — double check dairy.
