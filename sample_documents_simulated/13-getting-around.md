# Getting Around: Transit, Parking, and the Off-Campus Food Map

## Campus shuttle
The free campus shuttle runs three loops — the Main Loop (academic core + central dorms),
the River Loop (out to Riverside Hall and the river dorms), and the Night Owl Loop. Track it
live in the BrightwoodTransit app; the printed schedules are always wrong. The **Night Owl
Loop runs until 2am** and does safety stops on request, which is the move if you're walking
back from the library or off-campus late. Daytime shuttles come every ~10 minutes, nights
every ~20.

## City buses
Your student ID is a free bus pass on the city transit system — tap your ID, no fare. The
Route 7 goes downtown and to the big grocery store; Route 12 hits the cheaper off-campus
apartments and the mall. This is how carless students do a real grocery run.

## Parking (if you have a car)
Parking is the eternal complaint. Resident permits are a lottery and the good lots fill;
most underclassmen end up in the **Stadium Lot**, which is a 12-minute walk or one shuttle
stop from the core. Don't park in faculty lots, they ticket aggressively and tow by week 3.
Honestly most students ditch the car after first semester and live on the shuttle + bus pass.

## Off-campus eats (the good part)
Just off the east gate on College Ave is the main strip:
- **Moonlight Diner** — 24-hour diner, the late-night/hangover institution.
- **Slice Night Pizza** — open till 3am on weekends, delivers to dorms.
- **Taj Express** and **Pho 88** — cheap, fast, the two best value sit-down spots.
- The **College Ave food trucks** — tacos, halal carts, and a crepe truck; cluster near the
  gate till ~1am on weekends.
Off-campus spots don't take dining dollars (cash/card only), but they're where you go when
you're sick of the halls or it's 2am and everything on campus is closed.
