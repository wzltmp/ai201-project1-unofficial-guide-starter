Excerpt from The Michigan Daily's "Campus Culture reviews: Dining hall edition."

South Quad is widely considered the best — the pinnacle of U-M on-campus dining. It has
the widest range of stations and is home to the only kosher kitchen on campus, alongside a
halal station. There is a well-stocked salad bar, fried pita chips, a self-serve ice cream
machine, and comfort foods like pizza. One reviewer built "a macadamia white chocolate chip
and cherry sundae" from the ice cream station. Vegetarian options exist but often require
creative combinations of sides and hot sauce. Gloria, a longtime greeter, said her favorite
part of the job is "the men and women who walk in here every day."
