# #new-students — "how do I find clubs?"

**orientation_olivia:** the big one is the **Activities Fair** in the first two weeks of fall
semester, out on the main quad. literally every club tables there, you walk around and sign up
for email lists. don't overcommit — sign up for 8, actually go to 2. that's normal.

**robotics_ravi:** if you miss the fair, every club is listed on **BrightwoodConnect** (the
student org portal) with meeting times and a contact. you can join anytime, most clubs don't
care that it's mid-semester. the portal is also how you find the niche stuff that doesn't
table — there are like 300 registered orgs.

**culture_clara:** the cultural and identity orgs are some of the most active and the easiest
way to find community fast — they do food events constantly (which, given this server is next
to #food, you'll appreciate). Hillel and the Muslim Student Association also double as your
plug for kosher/halal dining info btw.

**intramural_ian:** don't sleep on **intramural sports** — you don't need to be good, teams
form every season through the rec center portal, and it's the easiest low-commitment way to
meet people outside your dorm. there's everything from soccer to inner-tube water polo.

**orientation_olivia:** real talk the clubs that change your life are usually the small ones
you find by accident. go to the fair, say yes to a couple things, drop the ones that don't
stick. by october you'll have found your people.
