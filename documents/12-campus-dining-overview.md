Excerpt from the University of Michigan Online Visitor's Guide to campus dining.

Michigan Dining is "one of the largest collegiate food service operations in the nation,"
serving "4,130,483 Dining Hall Meals Per Year" and employing "more than 1,000 students."
The campus has "seven full-service residential dining halls and a network of 22 on-campus
cafes, markets, and grills" — including the Blue Market at Bursley, the Blue Cafe at East
and South Quad, and The Pantry at Mary Markley. "Most offerings are made from scratch."
Every item carries nutrition and allergen information, with icons for "MHealthy,
gluten-free, halal, carbon, kosher, vegan, and vegetarian," and there are "gluten-free
pantries in every dining hall." Walk-in visitor groups of five or fewer are welcome during
regular hours.
