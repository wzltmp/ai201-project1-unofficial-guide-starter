# Where do you actually get work done? Study spots ranked

**u/grind_mode (OP):** Ranking the study spots by what they're actually good for, because
"the library" is not a single place.

**For deep quiet / late-night focus:**
- **Tewell Library, 3rd and 4th floors.** These are the designated **quiet floors** — silence
  enforced, individual carrels, no talking. This is the best place on campus to study late at
  night with zero distractions. The 4th floor is the quietest spot on all of campus. Library is
  open until 2am on weekdays during the semester, 24 hours during finals.
- **The 24-hour Engineering lounge** (Voss Hall, ground floor). Literally never closes. Has a
  coffee machine, whiteboards, and outlets everywhere. The go-to for all-nighters when even the
  library closes. Gets a little gross by 4am but it's there.

**For group work / collaboration:**
- **Tewell Library 1st and 2nd floors.** Talking allowed, reservable group rooms with screens.
  Loud-ish but that's the point. Book the group rooms in advance during midterms, they go fast.
- **The Greenhouse** (the glassed-in atrium by the science buildings). Social, sunny, plants
  everywhere, decent for group work or chill solo work, but it is NOT quiet — people treat it
  as a hangout. Good vibes, bad for a focus grind.

**For "I need coffee and a table":**
- **Grindhouse Café** in the library ground floor — caffeine + tables, but it closes ~9pm.
- Bean Scene in the union if you want food too.

**u/nightowl_nick:** Hard agree on Tewell 4th floor being the quiet GOAT. For late nights I
do Tewell quiet floors until 2am, then move to the Engineering lounge if I'm still going.
The Greenhouse is great but I can't get anything done there, too many friends walk by.

**u/grind_mode:** TL;DR — quiet/late = **Tewell 3rd–4th floor** or the **24-hr Engineering
lounge**; group = Tewell lower floors; social = Greenhouse.
