# Is Hillside Commons actually good or do people just go because it's central?

**u/quadrat_kid:** Hillside is fine. The food is solidly average — the rotating
entrées are hit or miss but the salad bar and the pasta station carry it. The real
problem is the lunch rush. If you show up between 12:00 and 1:00 you are looking at a
15 to 20 minute line just to swipe in, and then every seat is taken. Go before 11:30
or after 1:00 and it's a completely different building. Honestly the wait is the #1
complaint everyone has about Hillside.

**u/tofu_scramble:** Seconding the lunch rush warning. I have a class that gets out at
12:15 right next to Hillside and I stopped going because by the time I got through the
line I had ten minutes to eat. Now I go at 11:20 between classes and it's empty. The
breakfast there is genuinely underrated — the omelet station guy is fast and the lines
are nothing before 9am.

**u/just_here_4_food:** People sleep on the fact that Hillside has the longest weekday
hours of the central halls, open 7am to 9pm. So even if lunch is a zoo, dinner around
7:30 is calm and you can actually find a table. Quality-wise I'd call it a 6/10 — never
amazing, never inedible. The dessert station is the best part, the cookies are made in
house.

**u/commuter_caroline:** As a commuter I only get the central halls between classes and
Hillside's lunch line genuinely eats into my schedule. I wish they'd open the second
swipe lane during peak. The food is fine. The crowding is the issue, not the menu.

**u/quadrat_kid:** Update for the freshmen reading this: the trick is the side entrance
by the courtyard has its own swipe station that nobody uses. Cuts the lunch wait in half.
