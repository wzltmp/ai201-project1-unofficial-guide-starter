# The Marketplace: is it worth using a swipe or paying out of pocket?

**u/budget_brain:** The Marketplace is the food-court-style spot — it's not all-you-can-eat
like the halls, it's individual stations (grill, grain bowls, pizza, a grab-and-go fridge)
where you pay per item with dining dollars or a meal swipe. One meal swipe = one combo
(entrée + side + drink), which is a good deal at the grill but a waste at the grab-and-go
fridge where a sandwich is cheaper in dining dollars. Do the math before you swipe.

**u/late_plate:** The grab-and-go fridge at The Marketplace is clutch for late nights —
pre-made sandwiches, wraps, yogurt, fruit. The hot stations close at 8pm but the fridge
and the convenience corner stay open later, so when the dining halls are closed you can
still grab something here without going off campus.

**u/quadrat_kid:** Best value at The Marketplace is the grain-bowl station, hands down.
You get a huge bowl for one swipe and it's actually healthy. The pizza is whatever. The
lines here are shorter than the all-you-can-eat halls because it's spread across stations.

**u/commuter_caroline:** As a commuter The Marketplace is where my dining dollars go
furthest because I can buy exactly one thing instead of paying full price for an
all-you-can-eat swipe I won't finish. If you're on a block or commuter plan, learn the
Marketplace prices — it's the most flexible spot on campus.

**u/budget_brain:** One warning: dining dollars and meal swipes are different buckets.
Swipes don't roll over week to week on most plans, dining dollars do. Use your swipes at
the all-you-can-eat halls or the Marketplace combo, and save dining dollars for the fridge
and coffee.
