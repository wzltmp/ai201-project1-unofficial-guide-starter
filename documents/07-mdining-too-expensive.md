Excerpt summarizing The Michigan Daily opinion column "My beef with MDining."

A student opinion column argues that MDining is overpriced for what students actually get.
A recurring complaint is fairness: some dining halls, such as South Quad and Mojo, are
significantly better than others, which feels unjust given that every student pays the same
high meal-plan cost regardless of which hall is closest to them. North Campus students —
effectively limited to Bursley — feel this inequality most acutely. The column ties the
high fixed cost of the plan to uneven quality across halls. (See also the Daily's data
piece "The cost of eating at UMich.")
