Excerpt from The Michigan Daily's "Campus Culture reviews: Dining hall edition, Part 2."

Bursley, the main dining hall on North Campus, drew the most critical review. "The Bursley
Dining Hall, nestled in the heart of North Campus, feels busy at best and overwhelming at
worst." The biggest complaint was the limited number of stations: "Why were there only four
measly areas to get food, as compared to the up-to-10 that usually populates other dining
halls?" The reviewer also noted small paper plates that were inadequate for a full meal and
a strong composting/waste presence. North Campus students are largely stuck with Bursley
given the distance to the Central Campus halls.
