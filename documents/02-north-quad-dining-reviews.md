# North Quad appreciation thread (the stir-fry station is elite)

**u/greenhouse_gremlin:** North Quad is the best dining hall for anyone who doesn't eat
meat, full stop. The stir-fry / wok station lets you build a tofu and veggie bowl to
order and it's the single most reliable hot vegan meal on campus. Every other hall makes
you hunt for sad steamed vegetables; here you get a real cooked meal. The tofu is
actually pressed and crispy, not the rubbery cubes you get elsewhere.

**u/late_plate:** The other underrated thing about North Quad is the hours. It's the
latest-closing dining hall on campus — open until midnight on weeknights when everything
else shuts at 8 or 9. If you have a night lab or you're a 10pm dinner person, North Quad
is basically your only real hot-food option. It gets busy around 9pm with the post-study
crowd.

**u/tofu_scramble:** Can confirm on the vegan thing. As a vegetarian North Quad is the
only hall I trust to have a hot option that isn't just the side vegetable. The stir-fry
line moves slow during peak because everything is made to order, so budget time, but it's
worth it. They also do a tofu scramble at weekend brunch that is genuinely good.

**u/meatlover99:** Counterpoint for the carnivores — North Quad's grill is mediocre and
the burgers are dry. If you want meat go to Hillside or The Marketplace. North Quad's
whole strength is the veg/vegan stuff and the late hours. Play to that.

**u/greenhouse_gremlin:** Pro tip: the stir-fry station closes about 30 minutes before
the rest of the hall, so if you're coming for the late-night hours specifically for the
wok, get there before 11:30, not 11:55.
