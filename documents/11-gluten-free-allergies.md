Excerpt from the MI Gluten Free Gal guide to food-allergy dining at U-M.

U-M operates separate, locked-down gluten-free rooms at South Quad and Bursley: "This
separate locked-down room is stocked with gluten-free and other free-from foods," each with
"its own set of plates, silverware & cups, its own dishwasher, and even freshly prepared
foods." Students gain access through their MCard after completing online cross-contact
training. Beyond the dedicated rooms, "All dining halls have gluten-free pasta and some sort
of dedicated space where gluten-free options are available." At the 24 Carrots pizza station
staff "make it in a separate pan with separate toppings," and at Wildfire they prepare "a
burger in a dedicated pan and separate area." A university database of more than 10,000
recipes is flagged with allergen information.
