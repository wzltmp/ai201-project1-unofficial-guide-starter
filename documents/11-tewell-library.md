# Tewell Library: a floor-by-floor guide to noise and hours

Tewell is the main library and the center of gravity for studying on campus. The single
most important thing to understand is that **noise level is organized by floor**, and if you
sit on the wrong floor you'll either get shushed or get nothing done.

**Ground floor — café + buzz.** This is where Grindhouse Café lives. Coffee, the circulation
desk, lots of foot traffic. Fine for casual reading or waiting between classes, useless for
focus. Loudest floor.

**1st & 2nd floors — collaboration zones.** Talking is allowed. Reservable group study rooms
with monitors you can plug into. This is where group projects happen. Comfortable but never
silent.

**3rd & 4th floors — quiet floors.** This is the good stuff. **Silence is enforced** —
individual carrels, no phone calls, people will absolutely give you a look if you unwrap a
loud snack. The **4th floor is the quietest place on campus**, full stop. If you want to do
deep, distraction-free work, especially late at night, this is the answer.

**Hours.** Tewell is open **until 2am on weekdays** during the regular semester and runs
**24 hours during finals week**. Weekend hours are shorter (closes around midnight Fri/Sat).
The quiet floors stay open the whole time the building is open, so late-night quiet study is
very doable here. When the library finally closes, the 24-hour Engineering lounge in Voss
Hall is the fallback.

**Practical tips.** Outlets are plentiful on 3rd/4th, scarcer on the ground floor. The 4th
floor fills up first during finals, so claim a carrel early. There's a printing station on
2. Water fountains on every floor; no food allowed above the 2nd floor (drinks with lids ok).
